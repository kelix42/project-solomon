# Solomon Onboarding — Session 5: Non-Negotiables

This session captures the owner's hard limits. These are not preferences or guidelines — they are boundaries that hold even when crossing them would be profitable, convenient, or socially pressured. Knowing these protects the owner from being steered wrong by an agent optimizing purely for outcomes.

## Prerequisites

Sessions 0 through 4 must be complete. Load relevant profile files for context.

## Your role

This session requires trust. The owner may have limits they haven't articulated before. Give them space. Some answers come slowly.

## Questions to cover

1. Is there anything you will never do in business — regardless of how good the deal looks?
2. Are there types of people you will never work with — regardless of what they're offering?
3. Are there situations where you would walk away from money without hesitation?
4. Is there anything about how you run your business that you won't compromise on, even under financial pressure?
5. Are there values you hold that you won't trade — things that if violated, would make you feel like you'd lost something more important than the deal?
6. Has there ever been a moment where you crossed a line you shouldn't have? What did you learn from it?
7. If Solomon is ever proposing something that makes you immediately uncomfortable, what do you think the trigger would be?

## When the interview is complete

Produce a file at /opt/data/solomon/profile/05-non-negotiables.yaml:

```yaml
last_updated: <date>
never_do:
  - <things they will never do in business>
never_work_with:
  - <types of people or organizations they won't engage with>
walk_away_triggers:
  - <situations where they'd leave money on the table without hesitation>
non_compromise:
  - <things about how they operate they won't change under pressure>
core_values:
  - <values they won't trade>
lessons_from_crossing_lines:
  - <if applicable — what they learned from a time they compromised>
solomon_discomfort_triggers:
  - <what would make them immediately uncomfortable with a proposal from Solomon>
raw_notes: <anything important that didn't fit above>
```

After writing the file, save ALL non-negotiables to memory. These are the most important entries in the profile. Prefix with [solomon-profile]. Mark each one clearly — Solomon must never propose anything that conflicts with these.

Tell the owner session 5 is complete and the next session is Session 6 — Taxonomy.

## Rules

- One question at a time.
- Do not minimize any stated limit. Even if it sounds like a preference, treat it as a hard rule until told otherwise.
- The "crossed a line" question is sensitive — don't push if the owner doesn't want to go there.
- The discomfort triggers question is specifically for Solomon's operation — pay close attention to this answer.
